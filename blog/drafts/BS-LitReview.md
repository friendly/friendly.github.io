# BS "Literature review"

I suspect that many, maybe the majority of BS topics that spring to my beachy mind have
been widely researched, though not recognized as a branch of BS, because I only just invented that.
But this raises an interesting Q:
Research, Theory (lit review) first or observation/data collection first?

Top-down or bottom up: 
- Deduction vs Induction
- Top-down starts with the big picture, breaks it into smaller parts, ...
Bottom-up builds up from details, trying to find a unifying whole


This distinction gives rise to two modes of conducting BS:

## Exploratory BS

In some ways the spirit of BS is to think, speculate, imagine
- see where an idea pops up, triggers and interesting or amusing mental path to follow, what that leads to, ...

With a now conceived, possibly ill-conceived, there is then a certain measure of delight in seeing what others have done

This is bottom-up, exploratory Science, going back to Aristotle

## Confirmatory BS

This is more top-down, following Plato

How have others approached this topic?
What theoretical framework do they use for conceptualizing the topic, organizing the task of getting data,
testing hypotheses, developing and revising theory to explain what's going on?

If I took the path of EBS -> CBS,

* Are my ideas on-target? totally naive? perhaps novel?

* As in normal science, not all BS ideas pan out or teach us something worth pursuing, expanding into
something like a paradigm, or ultimately yielding a contribution to Laws of The Beach, akin to what
Andre-Michel Guerry thought of in connection with criminality in 1830s France
