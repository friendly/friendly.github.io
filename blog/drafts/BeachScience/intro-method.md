## Beach Science Methods

Poincaré wrote that it is by logic that we prove, but by intuition that we discover. Beach Science leans hard on the second half of that. The posts in this series begin not with hypotheses but with noticing — a shadow, a sound, a discomfort, a question that arrives unbidden while doing nothing useful. The logic, the data, the models come afterward, in service of what curiosity already suspected.

This is, of course, the older tradition. Aristotle vs. Plato; Tukey's exploratory analysis before confirmatory; the naturalist's notebook before the laboratory. The beach is an especially good place for it. You can't bring much apparatus to the shore, but you can pay attention.

"What we observe is not nature itself, but nature exposed to our method of questioning."
- Werner Heisenberg, Physics and Philosophy: The Revolution in Modern Science (1958)
