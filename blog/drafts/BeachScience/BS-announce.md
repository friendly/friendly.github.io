# Beach Science announcement email

---

Subject: Beach Science: a new blog series (with actual science, mostly)

Dear colleagues,

I am writing from the start of my sabbatical where I've been on the Catalan
Mediterranean coast of France and the beaches of Menorca. Something interesting
can happen when a QM person lies down on a beach towel: scientific questions
can arise from the beach if you open your mind to them.

The result is a new blog series: **Beach Science**, maybe even a new scientific domain
with its' own methods. It is the practice of asking
fundamental scientific questions while horizontal, minimally dressed, and
within earshot of the sea. It is part field notes, part physics, part
statistics, R graphics as the occasion arises. and part an attempt to explain why the sand looks like the Sahara
and the shallow water is full of shimmering light.

Five posts are up so far:

1. [Beach Science: A New Field is Born](https://friendly.github.io/blog/posts/2026-06-beach-science/) ---
   the founding principles and methods of the discipline (June 12)

2. [Warm-Up Exercises for the Beach Scientist](https://friendly.github.io/blog/posts/2026-06-beach-science-warmup/) ---
   four exercises for becoming properly horizontal, attentive, and curious
   (June 14)

3. [BS: How Warm Is "Warm"?](https://friendly.github.io/blog/posts/2026-06-beach-science-howwarm/) ---
   temperature words are doing quantitative work while pretending to be
   qualitative; data visualization would like to fix this (June 21)

4. [BS: Are You a Beach Person or a Pool Person?](https://friendly.github.io/blog/posts/2026-06-beach-science-beachpeople/) ---
   a psychometric instrument disguised as a lifestyle decision (June 23)

5. [Beach Patterns as a Physics Laboratory](https://friendly.github.io/blog/posts/2026-06-beach-science-patterns/) ---
   sand ripples and sunlit caustics: two beautiful patterns, one deep
   principle (June 27)

More posts are in various stages of field research. The series lives at:
https://friendly.github.io/blog/

I hope you find something in here that makes you look at the beach differently.
Or at the very least, that it gives you something to read on the way there.

-Michael
