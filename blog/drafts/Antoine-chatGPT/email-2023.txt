In echo to our former exchanges on ChatGPT  I would like to send a non-serious contribution (but the deadline is possibly passed). The first pamphlet against ChatGPT (possibly the Encyclopedists) was published in 1759 by Pierre-Jean Migneret (17..-18..): Invention d'une manufacture et fabrique de vers, au petit métier, ou l'Art de versifier par les seules règles du calcul numérique, Arkstée & Merkus (Amsterdam) 1759 (See https://iris.univ-lille.fr/handle/1908/5106). Publishers names are faked as is the address and the book was certainly printed in France.  (Nevertheless, Kasper Arkstee et Hendrick Merkus existed and published in Amsterdam and Leipzig!) Pierre-Jean Migneret appears as a former mathematics teacher and accountant in a book which he published in Paris in 1798/1799 or an VII (La science des jeunes négocians et teneurs de livres; ou cours complet d'instructions élémentaires sur les opérations du commerce en marchandises et banque). In his1759 pamphlet, Migneret "claims" that by the use of dirty arithmetic (with a mix of coding and ad nauseam iterations of the nine rule proof principle), the algorithm answers simple questions. Two examples are worked out.  Example 1; question: Celui que j’aime deviendra-t-il mon époux ? answer: Ecce equidem liccitè praedicit talia numen. Example 2; question: La paix sera-t-elle prochaine et avantageuse aux Français? answer: Credo satis licitè, donabit foedera numen ...
