The first pamphlet against ChatGPT (possibly the Encyclopedists) was published
in 1759 by Pierre-Jean Migneret (17..-18..): Invention d'une manufacture et
fabrique de vers, au petit métier, ou l'Art de versifier par les seules règles
du calcul numérique, Arkstée & Merkus (Amsterdam) 1759 (See https://iris.univ-
lille.fr/handle/1908/5106). 

Publishers names are faked as is the address and the
book was certainly printed in France.  (Nevertheless, Kasper Arkstee et Hendrick
Merkus existed and published in Amsterdam and Leipzig!) 

Pierre-Jean Migneret
appears as a former mathematics teacher and accountant in a book which he
published in Paris in 1798/1799 or an VII (La science des jeunes négocians et
teneurs de livres; ou cours complet d'instructions élémentaires sur les
opérations du commerce en marchandises et banque).

Example 1; question: 

Q: Celui que j’aime deviendra-t-il mon époux ? 
A: Ecce equidem liccitè praedicit talia numen. 

Translations:
Q: Will the one I love become my husband?
A: Behold, indeed, the god predicts such things.


Example 2; 
Q: La paix sera-t-elle prochaine et avantageuse aux Français? 
A: Credo satis licitè, donabit foedera numen ...

Translations:
Q: Will peace be near and advantageous to the French?
A: I think it's fair enough, the god will give a covenant...
